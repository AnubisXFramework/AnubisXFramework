## 1. Methodology

Experimental validation was conducted through static code analysis and targeted execution of the Anubis Twitter Intelligence Prototype v2.5. Each experiment tests a specific hypothesis derived from the framework's theoretical claims. Results are assessed against criteria for implementation completeness, functional correctness, empirical support, and reproducibility.

## 2. Results Summary

A series of experiments were executed on a dataset of accounts from a specific demographic population. The experiments covered fingerprint extraction consistency, similarity search accuracy, cross-user differentiation, lexical feature analysis, demographic content verification, and text normalization.

### Outcome Distribution

The majority of experiments demonstrated functional feasibility of prototype components. A subset of experiments exhibited degraded performance or were blocked by external dependency issues. One experiment revealed an architectural inconsistency requiring resolution. Several experiments were assessed as publication-ready for feasibility or methodology venues.

## 3. Publication Readiness Assessment

### 3.1 Claims Publication Status

Scientific claims were assessed for publication readiness. Claims related to fingerprint construction feasibility, demographic filtering methodology, network analysis algorithms, and system architecture demonstrated sufficient maturity for methodology or feasibility publications. Claims dependent on multi-model ensemble evaluation, temporal analysis, cross-platform attribution, and multi-modal fusion require additional implementation or data collection.

A majority of core claims were assessed as publication-ready for feasibility or methodology publications. The remaining claims require additional implementation or data collection.

## 4. Statistical Analysis

### 4.1 Cross-User Similarity Distribution

Preliminary analysis of user fingerprint similarity distributions was performed. Complete statistical characterization requires additional data collection and resolution of architectural inconsistencies.

### 4.2 Lexical Feature Distributions

Analysis of lexical features including diversity metrics, complexity measures, and usage frequency patterns revealed account-dependent variation in writing style characteristics. Detailed distribution analysis requires resolution of external dependency issues.
