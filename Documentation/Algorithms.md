## 1. Overview

The framework employs a comprehensive set of algorithms organized by behavioral domain. Each algorithm addresses a distinct aspect of behavioral evidence processing: feature extraction, signal decomposition, profile construction, comparison, evidence fusion, confidence estimation, similarity computation, and decision logic. The catalog reflects the theoretical foundations of behavioral identity attribution as a multi-step evidential reasoning process.

---

## 2. Algorithm Domains

The framework processes behavioral evidence through several conceptual stages:

- **Core Processing**: Fundamental operations on behavioral data — extracting behavioral signals from observations, constructing evidence records, and updating internal representations.
- **Behavior Processing**: Decomposition of observed behavior into constituent components (cognitive, contextual, temporal, and noise), profile construction, comparison, and normalization across contexts.
- **Identity Determination**: Estimation of cognitive centroids from multiple observations, identity matching against known profiles, profile aggregation, and identity verification.
- **Evidence Processing**: Accumulation of evidence over time, weighting by quality and relevance, fusion across sources, and temporal integration.
- **Attribution Scoring**: Likelihood ratio computation, multi-modal evidence combination, and attribution confidence estimation.
- **Confidence Estimation**: Confidence estimation from score distributions, temporal confidence decay modeling, confidence combination, and calibration.
- **Similarity and Distance**: Standard similarity and distance measures appropriate for high-dimensional behavioral feature spaces.
- **Multi-Modal Fusion**: Fusion of evidence from multiple behavioral sources using score-level, feature-level, and decision-level strategies.
- **Decision Logic**: Threshold-based decision making, threshold optimization, quality-based rejection, sequential evidence accumulation, and adaptive threshold adjustment.
